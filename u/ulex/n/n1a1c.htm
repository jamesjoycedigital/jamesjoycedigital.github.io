In Inisfail the fair there lies a land, the land of holy Michan. There rises a watchtower beheld from afar. There sleep the dead as they in life slept, warriors and princes of high renown. There wave the lofty trees of sycamore; the eucalyptus, giver of good shade, is not absent: and in their shadows sit the maidens of that land, the daughters of princes. They sing and sport with silvery fishes caught in silken nets: their fair white fingers toss the gems of the fishful sea, ruby and purple of Tyre. And men come from afar, heroes, the sons of kings, to woo them for they are beautiful and all of noble stem.

Gurnard & plaice those are. Speckled backs. One after another hook in their gills. Can't be hunger drives them. Probably curiosity. Curiosity killed the fish.
