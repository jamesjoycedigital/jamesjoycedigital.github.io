Professor Maghinni parts the curtains and presents himself. He wears a slate caftan frockcoat with claret silk lapels, a green lowcut waistcoat, stock collar with black white kerchief tie, tight lavender trousers, patent pumps, canary gloves. In his buttonhole he has an immense white dahlia, in his left eye a flashing eyeglass. He places his hand lightly on his chest and bows. Then minuets forward three paces.

Maghinni

Tout le monde en avant. Réverence. Tout le monde en place.

Behind the curtains Ponchielli's dance sounds, damped. The morning hours run out, goldhaired, slimsandalled, in robes of blue. The hours of noon follows, in amber gold.

Maghinni

(claps his gloved hands) Carré! Avant deux! Balancé.

The evening hours run out, dispersed, lagging: They are in grey gauze with darker bat sleeves.

Maghinni

Avant huit! Traversé.

The night hours stand to the last place. They are in black, masked, with daggered hair. Each has a bracelet of dull bells.

The Bells

Heigho! Heigho!

Maghinni

Saluts! Cours de mains! Croisé! Les Tiroirs! Chaine de Dames! La Corbeille. Dos à Dos. Boulangère. Les Ronds. Les Ponts. Escargot.

Arabesquing, they weave a pattern on the floor, curtseying, twirling, simply swirling, twining, receding, with interchanging hands, linked each to each, with arching arms, a mosaic of their movements.

They The veils fall, floating, fall.
