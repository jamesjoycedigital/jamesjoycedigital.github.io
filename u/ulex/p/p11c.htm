By no pomp of pride or mark of mightiness is the wellbeing of a people more surely proven than by tribute of solicitude for its own continuance and where this quality either is lacking or is set small store by we cannot with justice esteem a nation so little provident, however wide her realm, be she ruled never so wisely. For what boots it to gather and to garner for none, or weaklings shall come after? Or is that wisdom, to extol his meat above the man himself, the dead which serves for life above the living, which death should serve? Plainly nought and no. If care for progress be absent in vain were the rooftree raised or threshold laid.

Yet what boon of all can vye with the bounty of increase. Where it is found the land teems, where not, is bare: or the heath and hillside, this but that the abundant wheatfield of heavy awns, the fertile loam of fallows, the herds numbered and blessed. So was it commanded to the patriarch to increase and multiply and it was promised him his seed should not fail but be numerous: and even the pagans saluted on meeting amid the press a matron with child for, if command and promise from on high warrant and if the wise of mankind at all times and everywhere establish it, (and even the rudest know this awe), revered motherhood should be above all else and its abode a temple inviolate.

Such was, witness the writers of Story, the practice in our land from the earliest times where leechcraft was ever held high in honour. The sick, the wayfarer, the stricken were the objects of wise care. Not to speak of their hostels, their sweating chambers & leperyards and plaguegraves, their houses of sorrow, their greatest leeches: as the Shiels than whom none was wiser, the O'Lees and the O'Hickeys with many others, record in their books the lore of herbs and maladies with their cures as we may read with much instruction how the trembling hand was made whole and how the withering

Within the womb he won worship. A couch and food were set for her than should bear him and clean clothes and the service of midwives and what drugs were needed or surgical tools in the high bright wellbuilt fair home of mothers when, being then far gone, she drew thither to lie in, her term come.

Nor has this wont stopped short of our time. The hospitals of the Coombe, Rotunda of the National Maternity Hospitals, stand among us, where day following day is fostered the neverfailing fount of life so that the tale of creatures shall not ever lessen, for nature renews it of her bounty giving, if but to her one gift be given, an hundredfold yield. In this greater than we and in her strength greater for the earth will suffer that chasms rend her loins and the sea be sundered to her depths yet show no worse, so strong in them is nature, while her chief child faints under a light load, wailing comes to birth and is born bondchild into woe. But woe unto them that know not such woe. Sweet woe whereof joy comes. If hallowed the ground where lie our dead thrice hallowed be that house where life burgeons to new life, love bears fruit and flower.
