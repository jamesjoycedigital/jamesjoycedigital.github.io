But sir Leopold at that word was grave by cause he still had pity of shrillshrieking women that were with child and he was minded of his good lady that had borne him a manchild which in his eleventh day on live had died and she was stricken at heart that the babe was dead and to his burial, sore weeping, did him on a fair white corselet of lamb's wool flower of the flock lest he should perish utterly and lie like akale (for it was in midst of the winter) and now sir Leopold that had of his body no manchild for an heir looked upon him, his friend's son, and as sad as he was that him failed a son of parts and gentle courage so grieved he in no less measure for young Stephen for that he lived riotously and murdered his goods with whores.
