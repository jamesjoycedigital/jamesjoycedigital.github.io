— You have, of course, dear Shaun, read this letter for his majesty?

— Y I, replied Shaun, have.

Letter written by Sheme, brother of Shaun, for Alp, mother of Shaun, all about Hek, father of Shaun.

— Dear Shaun, I said, since you came to the use of language have you millions of times used worse language than that used by Shem.

— I doubt it, Shaun replied.

— You could come near it, dear Shaun, I suppose? I asked.

— No-one could, Shaun replied.

— But I suppose you could

use yourself yourself, dear Shaun, if you only took the trouble of so doing?

— Undoubtedly so, I could do it much worse than my brother Shem only I would never take the trouble, was Shaun's reply

Mind you he was in the deepest earnest but he overbalanced by weight of the barrel and rolled a fairish way in the Inchicore direction before being righted.

Well, dear Shaun, may the moss of all peace & prosperity unread your rolling home! May the foggy dews bediamond your hoops!? May the fireplug of filiality safeguard your bunghole!]We know you were loth to leave us. & sure, you will turn up some day when your pocket is empty. Anyway may the grass grow quickly under your feet or the daisies grow lightly over your toes.º1*
