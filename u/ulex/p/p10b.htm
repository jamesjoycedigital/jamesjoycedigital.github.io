But sir Leopold at that word was grave by cause he had for soreshrieking women and he was minded of his good lady that had borne him a babe son which in her eleventh day had died and she was griefstricken, and that the babe was dead and to his burial, sore weeping, did him on a fair corselet of woollen net lest he perish utterly (for it was winter) and now sir Leopold looke that had of his body no heir looked upon him, his son's friend's son, and as sad as he was that him failed a son of such gentle courage so grieved he for young Stephen that lived riotously and murdered his goods with whores.
