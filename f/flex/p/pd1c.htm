— Could you, of course, dear Shaun, I knew, while in the barrel read the strangelooking Shem language of this letter to His Majesty?

— I, replied Shaun, could play it like anything. But it is awful in its handwriting. It is a piece of scribble. A child sailing eggshells on the floor of a wet day would have more sabby

Letter written by Shem, brother of Shaun, for Alp, mother of Shaun, about Hek, father of Shaun.

— Dear Shaun, I said, since you came to the use of language have you not, without suggesting for an instant, millions of times used language ten times as worse as the language used up by your Shem.

— I would not like, Shaun replied, to swear just for the moment as to that, but I doubt it. She, the mother, was put up to it by him, the iniquity.

— Will you not, dear Shaun, I suggested, describe how?

— For three days, Shaun replied, she kept howling & bawling with him handling the pen with a hiccup. It all lasted seventy hours.

— What for, dear Shaun? I asked.

— For his language, Shaun replied, inside his letter.

— But You could come near it, dear Shaun, I suppose? I asked.

— No-one could, Shaun replied, for its being incendiary.
{ms, 13}

— Still I suppose you could use worse yourself dear Shaun, I supposed, if you only would take the trouble of so doing?

— Undoubtedly so, Shaun replied, & by the powers of war I could do it any time I liked even with my left hand as easy as a keg of beer and much worse than what my bogus brother Shem is conversant with only for, as an amateur & other things, I would never take so much trouble in doing so. And by all I hold sacred I swear my oath by the name of Shaun that I will commit to the flames any incendiary whoever will try to set a mother of mine on fire.

Mind you, he was in the deepest of earnest about it though his jaw was too heavy to talk a lot only, in looking up to find out what age he might be by the polar star, he overbalanced by the mighty fine weight of the barrel and, as the wisest course, rolled backwards round Sane's corner out of further earshot with his curious mode of motion a fairish way enough behind the times in the direction of

MacAuliffe's of Delgany before he was really uprighted.

Well, thou art passing hence, dear Shaun, from carnal relations and familiar places, more is the pity but for all your deeds of goodness may the moss of prosperousness gather you rolling home! May foggy dews bediamondise your hoops! May the fireplug of filiality safeguard your bunghole! We know you were loth to leave us but, sure, you will turn up some day when your pocket is empty and anyway in any case may the grass grow quickly under your feet or the daisies grow lightly over your toes!º
