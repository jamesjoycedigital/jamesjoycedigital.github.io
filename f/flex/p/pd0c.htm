— Could you, of course, dear Shaun, read this strange language in the letter for his majesty?

— I, replied Shaun, cd play it. It is awful in its handwriting. A piece of scribble.

Letter written by Sheme, brother of Shaun, for Alp, mother of Shaun, about Hek, father of Shaun.

— Dear Shaun, I said, since you came to the use of language have you millions of times used worse language than that used by your twin brother Shem.

— I wd not like to swear but I doubt it, Shaun replied. She was put up to it by him.

— You could come near it, dear Shaun, I suppose? I asked.

— No-one could, Shaun replied, for it is incendiary.

— But I suppose you could
{ms, 6}
use worse yourself yourself, dear Shaun, if you only took the trouble of so doing?

— Undoubtedly so, & by the powers of war I could do it any time I liked with my left hand tied much worse than any twin brother Shem only I would never take the trouble of doing it, was Shaun's reply And by all I hold sacred I swear an oath by the name of Shaun that I will commit to the flames any incendiary whoever tries to set a mother of mine on fire.

Mind you he was in the deepest of sluggish earnest though his jaw was too heavy to talk but in looking up to find what age he was by the polar star he overbalanced by weight of the barrel and rolled backwards in a curious mode of motion a fairish way behind the times in the direction of Delgany before being put right.

Thou art passing hence, dear unread, from friends and parents dear, but may the moss of peace & prosperity gather you rolling home! May foggy dews bediamond your hoops? May the fireplug of filiality safeguard your bunghole! We know you were loth to leave us but, sure, you will turn up some day when your pocket is empty. Anyway may the grass grow quickly under your feet or the daisies grow lightly over your toes.º
