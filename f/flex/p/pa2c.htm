Methought as I going asleep somewhen in nonland of wheres please I heard as 'twere the peal of midnight's chimes from out the belfry of the cute old speckled church sounding so faint a 12 as nighthood rendered all animated British objects nonviewable to human watchers save perchance anon some glistery gleam darkling adown surface of a fluvial flowandflow as again might be garments of laundry reposing a greensward close by in full expectation. And as I was going along in a dream as dozing I was dawdling methought broadmouth was heard and all vociferated, echoating: Shaun! Shaun! Post the post! With a high voice &, O, the higher his voice the deeper & low. I heard it so And lo, methought somewhat came of the noise & somewho seemed amove among allmurk, now 'twas as clump, now mayhap. When lo was light and now 'twas as flasher now more as the glow. Ah! in unlitness 'Twas, in verity 'twas his belted lamp. Yes, he who swayed a will of a wisp before me, dressed like an earl, in just the correct wear, with a classy o'coat of superior ruggedness freeswinging from one shoulder & thickwelted brogues on him made to suit the Irish people and climate & his coat of woollies with green & yellow effects out of it & a softrolling lapel to & the most successfully carried trousers you ever saw breaking over the ankle & hugging the shoeheel & his invulnerable burlap waistcoat and inside of it he was showing off his bluespangled zephyr overshirt that had a decidedly surplice short — everything the best, was none other from (Ah, then, the blessings of God & Mary &

Had I the concordant wiseheads of Messrs Gregory & Lyons along with Dr Tarpey's & the Rev. Mr MacDougall's but I, poor ass, am but as their fourpart ass. Yet methought Shaun (holy messenger angels be uninterruptedly nudging him among & along the winding ways of random ever!) Shaun in proper person (may all the backsliding constellations continue to be his changeable timetable!) was before me in more than his usual health and I tell you my word he looked the stuff. Now, without deceit it is hardly too much to say he was looking grand, so fired smart he was immense, quite swell and so jaunty with a schoolgirl complexion on him plainly out on the mash for he was after having a great time in a porterhouse where he had recruited his health by his three principal meals, his breakfast of a half a pound of bacon & eggs, then his dinner of a half a pound of round steak with some chops thrown in by the proprietoress and gravy with potatoes and a raw tomato and then finally his supper, videlicet, bacon with some cold breast of veal. I don't mean to say he was greedy but he liked his feed a lot. So Then he sproke When lo meheard I saw the voice of Shaun how so it sighed to scented nightlife as softly as the loftly marconimasts from Clifden sigh open tireless secrets to Nova Scotia's listing sisterpoles

— Alas alone alanna aroon! Shaun yawned, addressing himself & complaining of the fact of earning his bread in sweat of feet as, having moistened his mouth in quiet, scooped his teeth clean with his forefinger, as he sank down at once upon the native soil he loved covered with virgin bush. Well, I'm shot seeing myself like this! How all too unworthy am I, a mere mailman of peace, for such eminence

— But have we until now besought you, dear Shaun, we remembered, who it was, to begin with, who gave you the permit?

— Everybody, Shaun replied. It is my heaviest cross & I have it from Saint Columkille's prophecies

— Then, we explained, you might be so by order.

— Forgive me, Shaun replied with his liquid lips, it was condemned for me in the prophecies and there is a power over me that is put upon me from on high & so as it is I have nothing to look forward at and am hopeless to do anything about it.

— Honest Shaun, we agreed, a whisper reaches us that it may turn out to be you you who will maybe bear those open letter?

— As, Shaun replied patly, to that I have the power. And that has a lot to do with everything

— Would you mind telling us, Shaun honey, we proposed to that dear youth, where mostly are you able to work?

— I, Shaun replied, mostly am able to walk. I am always telling them how It was foretold for me to be disbarred from unnecessary servile work of all sorts work for otherwise I wd get into a blame. His Holy Will be done. But, believe me, I can truthfully declare with my hand on the epistles I am awful good & always I do my reasonable best to say my prayers regularly

— O, murder, Shaun replied. Well so be it & I will confess to have, yes, so long as I'm prepaid. And it wasn't my first time. Somebody may perhaps say I am wrong. But It is grandiose from the prophecies and they were particularly written by a gentleman for gentlemen. It was with my post lamp.

— Do you mean, we gathered, whether varnish will or verdure?

— It is a confounded lie to say it, bad luck to you, Shaun, the firy boy, replied, naturally very incensed, Another time Please confine your glaring insinuations to some other body What on the face of this earth wd I be doing besides your varnish? That is more than I can say anyway. So let you & I drop that. Understand me when I tell you that in the past parcel office, so much deplored by my erstwhile cousin friend, Miss Sanders, postmistress in general to the Irish Goat Society, albeit blessed with 22,000 sorters out of a biggest possible 22,000, too much administrative stationery was eaten by those goats. It is also one of my avowed intentions at some time when I am not prepared to say to compose quite a patent savings book surrounding that matter.
