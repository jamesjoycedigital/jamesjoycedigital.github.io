Hark! Twelve two eleven cater ten sax. Hark! Pedwar pemp. Five three. Twelve. Methought 'twas the peal of midnight's chime from out the belfry of the cute old speckled church, sounding so faint a twelve as darkness rendered all British objects nonviewable to human watcher save perchance anon some glittery gleam adown surface of the fluvial flowandflow or again it might sundry articles of laundry reposing upon the greensward hard by in full expectation. And methought broadmouth was heard and all vociferated, echoating: Shaun! Shaun! Post the post! And lo methought somewhat seemed amove amongst the murk, now 'twas as a clump, now mayhap. When lo there was light and now 'twas a flasher anon more as the glow. Ah, 'twas, in verity 'twas, his belted lamp. And he who swayed before me, will o' the wisp, coat on shoulder, was none other than (the blessing of God & Mary & Patrick be all over him) other than (may his hundred thousand welcome letters multiply & plultiply) Shaun himself.

Mr MacDougall's but I, poor ass, am but as their fourpart other ass yet methought Shaun (holy angels be uninterruptedly guiding him along the winding roads of random ever!) Shaun in proper person (may the constellations be his considering compass!) was before me and he spake. And lo meheard I saw the voice of him how it sighed to the scented nightlife as softly as the tall telegraph poleº at Clifden sigh open secrets to Nova Scotia listing sisterpoles.

— Alas, Shaun said, staring upon the soil he loved, I am all too unworthy for such eminence, or prominence, to be exact as to bear this letter on his majesty's service —

— But have I asked you, dear Shaun, I said, who it was gave you the permit?

— I, replied to me Shaun, have it from S. Columkille's prophecies.

— Then, I said, you might be so by order?

— Forgive me, Shaun replied, was made known in the prophecies and there is a power over me that is put upon me from on high.

— Dear, I said, then if it were you who will bear this letter you speak of?

— As, Shaun replied, to that I have the power.

— Tell me, I said, dear Shaun, where are you mostly able to work? I am mostly able to walk.

— It, Shaun replied, was foretold for
{ms, 5}
me never to work but, believe me, I say my prayers regularly.

— Yet one minute's conversation, dear Shaun, for as I observe, you have painted the town green.

— Well, yes, Shaun replied, With my post lamp which is grandiose.

— Do you mean, I insinuated, that verdure or varnish will?

— In, Shaun replied, the other postal office albeit blessed with 22,000 sorters out of a possible 22,000, too much administrative stationery was eaten by goats. It is also one of my avowed intentions at some time to make a book concerning that matter.
