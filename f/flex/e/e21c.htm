Majesty well Ive heard all these muckbirds what theyre bringing up about him and they will come to no good. The Honourable Mr Earwicker, my devout husband, and he is a true gentleman which is what none of the sneakers ever will be because as sings the royal poet
{ms, 37}
their likes must be born like he was, my devout, and it was between Williamstown and the Ailesbury road I first saw the lovelight in his eyes on top of the longcar I think he is looking at me yet as if he'd pass away in the clouds when he told me his true opinion to pardon him, golden one, but that I had got a lovely face and I felt I was back again in paradise lost when all the world was June.

Well, revered majesty, I hereafter swear never once he sent out the swags with a drop in them but milk as it came from the cow and that is all a pure makeup by a snake in the grass and his name is McGrath Bros against that dear man, my honorary husband. If I was to tell your revered all that caffler said to me was it this time last year as I told Mrs Tom for his accomodation McGrath Bros I'm saying and his bacon not fit to look at never mind butter which is forbidden by the 10 commandments thou shalt not bear false witness against thy neighbour's wife. Aha, McGrath, the lies is out on him like freckles. But I could read him. When I think what he had the shame to suggest about my dearly respected husband can I ever forget that? Never. So may the Lord forgive McGrath Brothers all his trespasses against the Honorary
{ms, 38}
Mr Earwicker. For 2 straws yes and less I could tell someone I know and they would make a corpse of him by private shooting with the greatest of pleasure and not leave enough of McGrath for the peelers to pick up.

Lies. There never was any girl in my house expecting trouble of my husband, never. The pair of whores that committed all the nuisance neither of them were virtuous pursuant to declaration of the public doctor out of the lock whereas I shall bring under revered noticeº above Honble Earwicker to possess a chest very hairy second to none for it to be able to be seen from a child which I am the privileged to see and pursuant to same very affectionate after salesladies' company. I will not have a dirty reptile the like of the McGraths to be spreading his lies all round where we live if he thinks he's the big noise here as I simply agree to it. There, you worm! I know you now I would hate to say what I think about him. I exgust sneak McGrath Bros wanting to live on me & my name like dirty parachutes. I wouldn't dream of a sausage belonging to him for meat for the cat & it was in all the papers about Earwicker's fatspitters that they were eaten by more than 15,000 persons in Dublin this weekend alone. The obnoxious liar! He was fired out of Cloon's where he was only one of your common floorwalkers for giving guff.

Moreover I've heard it stated about the military but did space permit it is my belief I could

Well, revered majesty, I tender you heartbroken thanks with regrets for lettering you and will now close hoping you are in the best. I don't care a fig for him and erronymous letter about an experience on the part of me as girl with a alleged unpleasant clerical friend. How about it? I was young & easy then. to feast his eyes on my sweet auburn hair falling on to my bare knees As it is my own by married woman's improperty act & I can do just as I please with it. Never mind poor Father Michael but answer my question if McGrath Bros was like him it wd be well for him When next you see M.G. Ask him what about his wife Lily Kinsella who became the wife of McGrath, with Mr John Brophy & Son, the kissing solicitor, at present engaging attention of private detectives being hidden under the grand piano to see whether nothing beyond handholding went on. Lily is a lady and she had medicine brought her in a licensed victualler's bottle. Shame! Thrice shame! I only wish he would look in through his letterbox one day and he would not say that was a solicitor's business.º What ho, she bumps! My, he would be surprised to see his old girl with Mr Brophy solicitor quite affectionate together, kissing and looking into a mirror.

So much for his sneakery

that I was treated not very grand by the thicks in Bully's acre. If any of Sully's thicks was to pull a gun on me he'll know better manners. I'll sully him. I will complain on them to P.S. Laracy at the corner of Buttermilk lane and he will have his head well & lawfully broken by a Norwegian who has been expelled out of Christianity.

Dear Majesty, I hope you are well, how are you? To speak the truth I was rather put out in my mind latterly about the thugs got up for McGrath by Sully. I am told he is at present in hospital with palpitations from all he drunk and it's seldom I saw him any other way. That he may never come out by he is a rattling fine bootmaker by profession.

I am perfectly proud of this great civilian, H.C. Earwicker, long life to him, my once handsome husband to be seen from my improved looks who is as gentle as a woman and a greatly attractable when he always sits fornenst me, poor ass, to make our polite conversations about lawful business & pleasure when he is after his 3rd or 4th mug of 4 ale & shag and he never chained me to a chair since this native island was born. & all the police and everybody is all bowing around to me when I go out in every direction & Earwicker is 100% human, I tell Sneakers and you Master McGrath, pale bellies, our mild cure, back and streaky, ninepence. I can show whoever likes the bag of one apiece cakes & Adam Findlater's best figrolls which was given to me on last occasion of golden wedding day by Mr Earwicker. Thank you, beloved, for your beautiful parcel. Always the born gentleman can be plainly seen by all from such behaviour. Then them to go & say about him being bothered as he possible could I must beg to contradict as indeed I may say he is after his manner & certified to be in the matter of his hearing very agreeably deef.

Well, revered majesty, I take the liberty of cherishing the expectation that the clouds will soon dissipate and will now conclude the above epistle with best thanks for your great kindest of all the trouble to took to regards for self & dearest of husbands Papa Earwicker who I'll be true to you as only lawful wife to unto my life's end so long as he has a barrel full of Bass & self to dear Maj & all at home
