— You are most kind as ever, dear sister, Shaun said. Till we meet to part no more.

He took from the gentle weeper the yellow label in which he dropped a tear, smothered a cough, spat a spit & blew his nose. He clapped the badge of belief to his brow, waved a hand
{ms, 8}
across the sea and toppled on his way to the left and was off and on his way.

Heaven speed you, rural Shaun, heart & soul of Shamrogueshire. Just by nature & natural by design you will be longed for & looked after while you are away. Life, it is true, is a dream without you. But you did your mile in swell style. And already the sombrer portion of the night is gone. W Brave, brave Shaun! Walk while you have the night for the day cometh when every post shall sleep.

(noises of morning)
