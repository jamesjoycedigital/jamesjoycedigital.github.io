Shaun, as I next saw, halted to take breath at the weir as he truly merited to do. He was propped up by against a slumbering clown who had fallen asleep there in the embrace of a bottle. There were several daughters there attracted, as I could see, by the sight who were paddling with their feet in charming concert with his snores. Among them Shaun easily recognised his dear sister, Izzy.

— Sister dearest, Shaun said in taking leave of her with fondness fondest affection, how you
{ms, 7}
will miss me but I feel I ought to go. This is the result of your teaching, Sis, that you used to write to me those nice letters. During my brief absence be true to the 10 commandments and preserve your dear chastity. Rather than part with that jewel of yours let the entire universe perish first. Oft shall I think of you when amid the music of the knockers. O how I shall kiss you immediately upon my return. Sis dearest, it is my grief to go on this errand but it is grand to be going to meet a king. One or two tears are all and then off we pop en route for His Blessed Majesty.

— Brother dear, Izzy said, accept this instead of a handkerchief and bear it with you always & when you use it think of the absent one.
