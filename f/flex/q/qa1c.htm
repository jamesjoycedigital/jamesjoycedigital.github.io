Good Shaun, as I was shortly before that made aware, next halted to take breath and loosen his bruised boots at the weir by Lazar's Walk about nine score or so barrelmoments' distance as truly he merited to do. He was there, I could see, perspiring but happy, propped up against a slumbering warden of the peace, one comestabulish Sigurdsen, who had fallen asleep at the curing station in the embraces of a confiscated bottle. Now, There were as many as 29 daughters out of the national hedge school
{ms, 15}
(for I seem to remember how it was a look before you leap year) learning the lesson of life there, seated as they were upon the brink attracted by the sight of the first human landmark, paddling with their eight and fifty pedals in charming concert to the snores of the log who seemed stuck to the sod as ever & anon he murmoaned, visibly unmoved, over his treasure trove of the crown: Dotter deadbedstead mean smuggy sort flasky. Shaun, after he had bowed to all the others in that chorus of praise of goodwill girls, asking kindly after their health, out of pure human respect for he was the most purely human creature that was ever called man easily made out his dear sister Izzy nor would he ever forget her as he was, besides that, her godfather as well after all.

— Sister dearest, Shaun delivered himself, as he began to take leave of her immediately with fondest affection, I honestly believe you will sorely miss me yet I feel, as a martyr to the discharge of duty, that I it is about time I would go. This is the result of your teachings in which I was raised, Sis, you, our angel in the house, that used to write to me the nice letters and tell me your oldworld tales of derringdo. During my brief absence be true to the ten commandments. Never miss your last mass. Never eat bad meat of a good Friday. Never let a hog of the hill trample on your lily of the valley. Never play ladies'
{ms, 16}
games on the Lord's day. Especially beware, please, of being a party to any demoralising home life. It saps a chap. No lowcut shirtwaists. Don't acquire a penchant for the fag habit. Look out for furnished lodgers paying for meals on tally with company & piano music the too friendly friend. Dip into the lives of the saints in weekly parts to better your mind. Keep cool above all your preserved chastity. Rather than part with that vestalite emerald of the first importance which you have where your two nether extremities meet, nay, let the entire ekumene universe perish to pot first a thousand times over in a pitfall. O be careful during this lenten pastoral season when the spring is in the making. Divulge the name & address of any lapwhelp that speaks to you on the street without a proper letter of introduction and as sure as I come back with the gravel spinning from beneath my feet rest assured I'll break his face for him. No, I'll tell you what I'll do. I'll burst his mouth Times and oft shall I think of you far away on your pillow when amid the music of the double doorknockers. Little girl from Liffeyside you fill a big corner in my heart. O, heaven, how I shall, if I live, put my arm in yours and kiss you immediately upon my safe return to ignorance & bliss when cherries come back when we shall show our kindness by adopting all the poorest children possible. We will render social service and clean up things. Look at the mud of Harrington street! Think of the banana peels all along Henry, Moore, Earl & Talbot streets. Stand on, say, Aston's Quay & take a good gaze into any shopwindow you like and in about 20 minutes' turn round & you will see you were covered with slush occasioned by the traffic in transit. Ask any disgusted lady cyclist who happened to be proceeding to or returning from her devotions during the bank holiday gale & downpour when pedestrianism was even difficult slates came freely off the best part of our grand old tenement houses what her laundry bill came to on last Monday & don't wait for the answer for if you were to do so, what would be the answer that wd meet your ears? Both. When will the longsuffering face of our beloved city get that longpromised wash? Or, better still, account to me for the fact that if you purchase any fish and vegetables you like from the same perambulator and having placed the mackerel, let us say, as well as the cauliflower on either of the edges of the two sidewalks, stand in some adjacent doorway to note the result the spots selected are visited in rotation by so many members of the canine tribe. Sis dearest, it is my grief to go away on this benedictine errand but it is grand to be going to meet a king, not a king only by surname, but the great king of Greater Dublin, the first Humphrey. I wish everyone was as sure of anything in the present world as I am of everything in the other to come. Tell mother that. And create no scenes. Now cheer up all round till the grim reaper comes as a blessing in disguise. No lonesome stuff. Drink it off, ladies, please. Parting's fun. Soon shall we be dead & happy together, as you all know. One or two tears are all there is to it and then in a click of the clock off we pop en route for His Blessed Majesty, our longlived Lord.
