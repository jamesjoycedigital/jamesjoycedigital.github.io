— Sir, to you. I am brought up under an old act of Edward the First but I am known throughout the world wherever good English is spoken as a cleanliving man & I think our public at large appreciates it most highly of me that I am cleanliving & as a matter of fact I possess the sweetest little wife on the globe who won the consolation prize in the dream of fair women's handicap by 2 breasts. She is my cherished wife and my private chaplain, an always sadfaced man who has visited our very hearts & reins, can speak to you some quite complimentary things of my clean character acting in the dark painful though such recital be to me. There is not a teaspoonspill of evidence to my bad & I must & do protest against future publication of libel by any Ticks Tipsylon to that highest personage at present holding down the throne. As a matter of fact I undertake to discontinue all practices
{ms, 115}
& honestly as my best policy I have had my lesson. The amusing part is I will say that since my toils of domestication began famine has receded from this land. Idle were it to inquire whether I be the mass product of team work or a forced outcome of group marriage for, surrounded by obscurity, I claim my natural born freeman's right to opt for Amen. I mean to say had my faithful Fulvia turned her back on her ways to go on the hills in search of brunette men or had she left her crocus bed at the mere suggestion of some infamous fishermen who wd have abused of her there might accrue advantage to ask but she ever did ensue the things that pertained unto fairness. and I did take the hand of my delights, my jealousy, & did lead her whimpering to Ringsend ferry & I did encompass her about, my verminbreeder, with all

lovingkindness & I gave unto my little wife turkeythighs soft goods & hardware & fine hosiery lines & geegaws such as women wear & all daintiness by teatime and I hung up my pigmy suns for the supperhour of my frigid one & I fed her upon spiceries for her garbage breath and to my saffronbreathing mongoloid I gave handewers & loinscrubbers & a currycomb for her tuzzy with clubmoss & wolvesfoot for her more moister places & I built for mine elskede, my shiny brow, an earth closet wherein to be quit of her sabbath & needs & I restored for her her paddypalace & added thereunto a shallow laver to put out her hellfire
and, rain or shower or drizzle or sleet, I did learn my little anna countrymouse her alphabeater cameltemper from ashenbirch to hazelyou

with my rattan at her drum ooah oyir oyir oyir & I did spread before my Livvy my selvage mats of softest lawn & I planted for my own hot lass a vineyard & fenced it about with Chesterfield elms & I brewed for her my first Dublin lindub to split the spleen of her maw & I lay down before the trotters of my eblanite my stonybattered waggonways whereon the mule & the hinny & the jennet & the mustard nag and piebald Shetlands & skewbald Orkneys stepped lively for her pleashadure & she laughed at the switchering of the whip.
