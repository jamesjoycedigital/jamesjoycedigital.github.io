Concerning the genesis of Harold or Humphrey Coxon's agnomen and discarding once & for all those theories from older sources which would link him either with such pivotal ancestors as the Glues, Gravys & Earwickers of Sidleham in the hundred of manhood or proclaim him a descendant of vikings who had founded or settled in Herwick (?) or Erwick (?) the most authentic version has it that it was this way. We are told how it came to pass that like cabbaging Cincinnatus in prefall paradise peace the grand old gardener was saving daylight one sabbath afternoon by following his plough for rootles in the rear garden of ye olde marine hotel when royalty was announced by runner to have been pleased to have halted itself on the highroad along which a dogfox had cast. Forgetful of all but his vassal's plain fealty to the ethnarch, Humphrey or Harold stayed not to yoke or saddle but stumbled out hotface as he was (his sweatful bandana loose from his pocketcoat) to the forecourts of his public in topee surcingle, plusfours and bulldog boots ruddled with red marl jingling his turnpike keys and holding aloft amid the fixed pikes of the hunting party a high perch atop of which a flowerpot was fixed earthside up. On his majesty, who was or feigned to be noticeably longsighted from green youth and had meant to inquire what had caused the causeway to be so potholed, asking to be put wise whether paternoster were not now more fancied bait for lobstertrapping honest blunt Harompheyld answered in no uncertain tones very similarly with fearless forehead: Naw, 'jsty, I war just a cotchin on them bluggy earwigs. Our sailor king, who was draining a gugglet of obvious water in his hand, upon this smiled very heartily beneath his walrus moustaches and, giving way to that none too genial humour which William the Conk on the spindleside had inherited from his great aunt Sophy, turned towards two of his retinue of gallowglasses, Michael, etheling lord of Leix in Offaly and the jubilee mayor of Drogheda (the two scatterguns being Michael Manning, protosyndic of Waterford and an Italian excellency named Giubilei according to a later version cited by the learned scholarch Canavan of Canmakenoise) and dilsydulsily remarked: Holybones, how our brother of Berengaria would fume did he know that weº have for trusty bailiwick a turnpiker who is also an earwicker. Comes the question Are these the facts as recorded in both or either of these andrewpomurphyc narratives? We shall perhaps not so soon see. The great fact remains that after that historic date all holographs initialled by Haromphrey bear the sigla H.C.E and while he was long and always good dook Umphrey for the hungerlean spalpeens of Lucalizod and Cox to his cronies it was as certainly a pleasant turn of the populace which gave him as sense of those letters the nickname Here Comes Everybody. Kelly's A Royal Divorce with ambitious selections from The Lily and The Bo Girl in the old playhouse on Chapel green on all gala nights from his viceregal booth where a veritable Napoleon IV the father of his people all the time sat with the entirety of his house about him with the invariable broadstretched kerchief cooling his whole neck and shoulders and in a wardrobepanelled tuxedo thrown completely back from a shirt wellnamed a swallowall in every way altogether outstarching the laundered clawhammers and marbletopped highboys of the pit stalls and early gallery. A baser meaning has been read into these letters the literal sense of which decency can safely only hint. It has been bruited by certain wisecracks that he suffered from a vile disease. To such a suggestion the one selfrespecting answer is to affirm that there are certain statements which ought not to be, and one would like to be able to add, ought not to be allowed to be made. Nor have his detractors, who, an imperfectly warmblooded race, apparently conceive him as a great white catterpillar capable of any and every enormity in the calendar recorded to the discredit of the Juke and Kellikek families mended their case by insinuating that, alternatively, he was at one time under the ludicrous imputation of annoying soldiers in the park. To anyone who knew and loved the Christlikeness of big cleanminded giant H.C. Earwicker throughout his existence the suggestion of him as a lustsleuth nosing for trouble in a boobytrap rings preposterous. But slander, let it lie its flattest, has never been able to convict that good great & no ordinary Southron Earwicker (as a pious author named him) of any graver impropriety than that advanced by certain woodwards and regarders, who did not deny that they had that day consumed their soul of the corn, of having behaved in ungentlemanly manner opposite a pair of dainty maidservants in the greenth of the rushy hollow whither, or so both gown & pinners alleged dame nature, as they alleged, had spontaneously and about the same hour of the eventide sent them both but whose combinations of testimonies are, if not dubiously pure, visibly divergent on minor points touching the nature of this, a first offence in vert or venison what was admittedly an incautious but, at its most, a partial exposure with attenuating circumstances of an abnormal S.
