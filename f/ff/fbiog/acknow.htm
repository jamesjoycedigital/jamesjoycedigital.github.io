Acknowledgements

A catholic procession of persons have to a lesser or greater degree assisted us in the slow gestation and delivery of this project, which originated in 1972 when Danis was invited by Tom Cowan, Professor of Roman Law, whom he had met by chance by sitting in an unoccupied seat (apparently reserved for Clive Hart, Professor of Literature) at a lecture on something or other during a James Joyce conference in Dublin, to spend some time with him and from this New York base go on to inspect the Joyce holdings, a large bunch of imperfectly organized manuscripts, at the State University of New York at Buffalo. On arrival at the Poetry Collection rooms, his research was facilitated by the gentleman-curator, Karl Gay, formerly secretary to Robert Graves, who encouraged his investigations into the Finnegans Wake notebooks there conserved, little-known in those years, made the originals and copies freely available to him, suggested some visual tricks useful in decipherment, and afforded him accommodation by day: space, table and chair. By night, when the Collection was inaccessible, Danis was most kindly looked after by Nan Bouton, who ran the students’ residence and who provided him with sustenance and a bed, and read poetry to him, and short stories. From these peripheral beginnings grew the ever-expanding hypertext project here concluded, as over the ensuing years we sought to make it evermore all-inclusive.
The litany of the various people who had helped us to push the business on is given below in two categories, academic and financial. We have tried to make it more or less chronological in arrangement, as individuals came into and departed out of our lives and work; while others remained.

We apologise for any omission: memory is imperfect.
