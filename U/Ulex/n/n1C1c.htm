The latter ducked but the Dubliner lifted him with a left hook, the punch being a fine one. The men came to grips and the bout ended with Bennett on the ropes and Myler punishing him. The Englishman was well drenched with water and when the whistle went came on gamey and full of pluck. It was a fight to a finish. The men fought like tigers and the excitement was terrific. After a rapid exchange of blows during which a clever upper cut of the military man brought blood freely from his opponent's mouth the lamb suddenly landed a terrific left to Bennett's stomach which floored him flat. It was a knockout clean & clever. Amid tense excitement the Shropshire boy was counted out and Myler declared the winner amid the frenzied plaudits of the public who broke through the ringropes and fairly mobbed him with delight.

The venerable patriot champion's stentorian notes were heard to the greatest advantage in the grand old song and both the timehonoured anthem itself and the superb highclass vocalism were vociferously applauded by the large audience among whom were to be noticed many prominent members of the clergy as well as representatives of the press and the bar and of the other learned professions.
